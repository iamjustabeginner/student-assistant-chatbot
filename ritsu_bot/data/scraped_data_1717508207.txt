<div class=main-contents><div class="column-group is-one"><div class=column-block><div class=mod-entry><div class="image-set is-center" style="max-width: 700px;"><figure><img alt="Campus Facilities" height=200 src="/image.jsp?id=246181&version=English" width=700></figure></div></div><h3 class="mod-title type3">Bank Accounts</h3><div class=mod-wysiwyg><p>All students are advised to open a Japanese bank account during their stay in Japan. Most institutions and organizations can only make deposits (scholarships, reimbursements, refunds, etc.) into Japanese bank accounts. Contact the appropriate bank or post office for procedure details.</p></div><h4 class="mod-title type4">Necessary documents and items required for opening a bank account</h4><div class=mod-wysiwyg><ol><li>Passport</li><li>Residence Card or National Health Insurance Card</li><li>Personal seal (a personal seal, "Inkan"in Japanese, is a stamp used in official documents in place of your signature)</li><li>Cash for deposit (minimum deposit varies depending on the bank)</li> Note: Students who have just arrived in Japan and do not yet have their Residence Card may be required to provide a Certificate of Residence, issued at your local city hall or ward office. </ol></div><h4 class="mod-title type4">Transferring money to your Japanese bank account</h4><div class=mod-wysiwyg><p>You will need the following information if you transfer money to your Japanese bank account. Please be aware that in addition to any fees your bank at home or any intermediary banks may charge you, a processing fee is assessed by the Japanese bank as well. This fee is usually JPY 2,500.</p><ol><li>Bank Name</li><li>Bank Code (4-digit)</li><li>Swift Code (8-letters)</li><li>Branch Name</li><li>Your Account Number</li></ol></div><h3 class="mod-title type3">ATMs</h3><div class=mod-wysiwyg><p>ATMs can be found at most banks, convenient stores, post offices and major department stores. Japanese ATMs are also capable of many different functions such as withdrawing money, depositing money, transferring money, updating your bank passbook, etc. To access these services you will need an ATM card and a pin number.</p></div><h4 class="mod-title type4">International ATMs</h4><div class=mod-wysiwyg><p>Japan Post Bank ATMs and Seven Bank ATMs allow you to use credit and debit cards issued outside of Japan. Please note that no other ATMs can access overseas accounts. </p></div><h4 class="mod-title type4">ATM Service Hours</h4><div class=mod-wysiwyg><p>The hours of operation will vary depending on the bank, but for the most part ATMs are available everyday from 9:00 to 21:00. Please keep in mind that a service charge may be applied depending on when you use the ATM. The best rates are usually obtained by going to large branches of major banks. Small branches usually do not have authorization to handle currency exchange. The bigger Japan Post Banks also offer exchange services at reasonable rates and may be open later than banks. Other locations include international airports, major hotels, and major department stores. </p></div></div></div></div>
